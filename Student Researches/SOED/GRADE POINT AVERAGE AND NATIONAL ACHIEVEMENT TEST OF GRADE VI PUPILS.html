GRADE POINT AVERAGE AND NATIONAL ACHIEVEMENT TEST OF GRADE VI PUPILS


GRADE POINT AVERAGE AND NATIONAL ACHIEVEMENT TEST OF GRADE VI PUPILS

By:
Jessa May B. Galicia
Myline O. Poseyo

March 2015


ABSTRACT

This study aimed to determining the level academic performance in Grade Point Average and National Achievement Test of Grade VI pupils of Lay-ahan Elementary School. The participants of this study were the Grade VI pupils. The average grade was used as data for Grade Point Average and the result of the National Achievement Test were used in this study. This present study employed the descriptive correlational method of research. The performance of the pupils in their grade Point Average is high in Hekasi with a mean of 85.45 and English as the least with a mean of 83.01, which are all under the description of approaching proficiency. The performance of Grade VI pupils in National Achievement Test was moving towards mastery in Filipino (80), Math (78.85), Science (69.69) and average in Hekasi (64.79) and English (64.58). Results revealed that there is no significant relationship between the level of performance in Grade Point Average and National Achievement Test of the Grade VI pupils as shown in the result of Spearman’s who which is .533 and is greater than .05 level of significance. Thus, null hypothesis is not rejected. It was concluded Grade Point Average is not correlated to the National Achievement Test performance.
